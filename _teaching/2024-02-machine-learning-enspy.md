Teaching Assistant for the Machine Learning course in the Financial Engineering track (3GIFA, first year of the engineering cycle), February 2024 to April 2024.

## Overview

Supported the delivery of a core Machine Learning course for first-year engineering students in the Financial Engineering track, bridging rigorous mathematical foundations with applied modeling for financial decision-making. The course emphasized end-to-end model development, from data preparation to evaluation and interpretation, with a focus on use cases relevant to banking, credit risk, and quantitative finance.

## Responsibilities

- Led weekly recitation and hands-on laboratory sessions reinforcing lecture material on supervised and unsupervised learning.
- Designed and graded problem sets, programming assignments, and the final project, providing detailed written feedback to students.
- Mentored student teams on applied projects, including credit scoring, fraud detection, and time-series forecasting of financial indicators.
- Held regular office hours to support students with Python implementation, model debugging, and conceptual questions.

## Topics Covered

- Supervised learning: linear and logistic regression, decision trees, ensemble methods (random forests, gradient boosting).
- Unsupervised learning: clustering and dimensionality reduction (PCA).
- Model evaluation, cross-validation, the bias-variance trade-off, and regularization.
- Practical workflow in Python using NumPy, pandas, and scikit-learn.

## Outcomes

Helped students translate theoretical concepts into working models, strengthening their ability to apply machine learning to real financial engineering problems and to communicate results rigorously.
